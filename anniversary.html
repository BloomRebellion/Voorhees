The Long Way Home

a record of the road we took to get here

March 2017 — ongoing

Before we were husband and wife…
Before we were Mom and Dad…
Before there was a place that finally felt like home…
There was just us.

On July 23, 2017, your dad asked me to be his girlfriend.

Neither of us knew that one simple "yes" would become years of winding roads, old vans, tiny motel rooms, first apartments, broken plans, unexpected kindness, unbearable loss, impossible hope, and a life neither of us could have imagined.

We weren't chasing an adventure.
We were just trying to build a life.

Some days we had no idea what we were doing. We made mistakes. We changed directions. We started over more than once. There were moments we had almost nothing except each other, and somehow, that was enough to keep us moving forward.

Then, somewhere along the way, the two of you came into our lives.
Jakob.
Elli.

You didn't arrive at the end of our story — you became the reason we wanted to remember it.

One day, you'll be old enough to read these pages and realize your family wasn't built in a single moment. It wasn't built on a wedding day, or in our first apartment, or even the day either of you were born.

It was built slowly.
One choice.
One mile.
One setback.
One laugh.
One act of kindness.
One ordinary day after another.

The pages that follow aren't perfect memories. They're real ones. Some are joyful. Some are painful. All of them matter, because together they became the life we share today.

Josh —

Thank you for saying "yes" to every version of our life, even the ones we never planned. If I could go back to July 23, 2017, knowing everything that was ahead of us, I would still choose you.
Every single time.

And Jakob and Elli —

When life takes you somewhere you never expected, I hope you'll remember where you came from. You come from a family that learned the long way that home isn't a destination.
It's the people who keep choosing one another, no matter where the road leads.

This is our story.
And it's still being written.

Nine years since he asked. Still logging.
