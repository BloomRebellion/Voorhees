Entry 01

The Jakob Sandwich

Inspired by Cheba Hut's White Widow, named after our firstborn.

Ingredients

  • 1 ciabatta roll
  • 1 boneless chicken breast, oven roasted
  • 2–3 slices bacon, cooked
  • 2 slices provolone cheese
  • Fresh tomato, sliced
  • Ranch dressing

Steps

  1. Toast the ciabatta roll.
  2. Layer the oven roasted chicken on the bottom half.
  3. Top with provolone and melt.
  4. Add bacon and fresh tomato slices.
  5. Finish with ranch and close the sandwich.
Entry 02

Easy Japanese Curry

Weeknight comfort food, made simple with store-bought curry roux.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb chicken thighs or beef stew meat, cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2½ cups water
  • ½ box (about 4 cubes) Japanese curry roux (e.g. Golden Curry)
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a large pot and brown the meat over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add carrots and potatoes, then pour in the water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer 15–20 minutes, until vegetables are tender.
  4. Remove from heat and stir in the curry roux cubes until fully dissolved.
  5. Return to low heat and simmer 5 more minutes, stirring, until thickened.
  6. Serve over rice.
Entry 03

Picadillo

A savory-sweet ground beef skillet, good over rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • ½ onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (8 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 potato, diced small
  • ⅓ cup raisins
  • ¼ cup green olives, sliced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooked white rice, for serving

Steps

  1. Brown the ground beef in a large skillet over medium heat. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in tomato sauce, potato, cumin, and oregano. Simmer 15 minutes, until the potato is tender.
  4. Add raisins and olives. Simmer 5 more minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper. Serve over rice.
Entry 04

Crispy Chocolate Chip Cookies

Thin, crisp-edged, and a little chewy in the middle.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 cups chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Whisk melted butter with both sugars until smooth.
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, and salt. Add to the wet mixture and stir until just combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips.
  6. Scoop tablespoon-sized balls onto baking sheets, spaced well apart. Flatten slightly.
  7. Bake 10–12 minutes, until edges are golden and centers still look slightly underdone.
  8. Cool on the sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
Entry 05

Fried Rice

The best use for yesterday's rice.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked, cold day-old rice
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil, divided
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • ½ cup diced carrots
  • ½ cup frozen peas
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat. Scramble the eggs, then remove and set aside.
  2. Add remaining oil, then carrots and garlic. Stir-fry 2 minutes.
  3. Add rice, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry 3–4 minutes until heated through.
  4. Add peas, green onions, and scrambled eggs back in.
  5. Drizzle with soy sauce and sesame oil, tossing to coat evenly.
  6. Cook 2 more minutes, then serve hot.
Entry 06

Grandma Mari's Fudge

Handwritten, handed down.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 tbsp butter or margarine
  • 2½ tbsp cocoa
  • ½ cup milk
  • 2 tbsp Karo syrup
  • ½ tsp vanilla

Steps

  1. Combine sugar, butter, cocoa, milk, and Karo in a saucepan.
  2. Beat/cook until it reaches soft ball stage.
  3. Cool, then beat again.
  4. Stir in vanilla.
